Call for Papers

Conference Tracks

ETMIS 2026 invites original research papers on topics related to Emerging Technologies for Multidisciplinary Innovation and Sustainability. The conference will feature the following tracks:

Track 1: Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning

  • Deep Learning and Neural Networks
  • Natural Language Processing
  • Computer Vision and Pattern Recognition
  • AI Ethics and Responsible AI
  • Machine Learning Applications
  • Reinforcement Learning

Track 2: Smart Systems and IoT

  • Internet of Things (IoT) Architectures
  • Smart Cities and Smart Grids
  • Industrial IoT and Industry 4.0
  • Edge Computing and Fog Computing
  • Sensor Networks and Embedded Systems
  • Wireless Communication Systems

Track 3: Sustainable Computing and Green IT

  • Energy-Efficient Computing
  • Green Data Centers
  • Sustainable Software Engineering
  • Cloud Computing Optimization
  • Carbon Footprint Reduction in IT
  • E-Waste Management

Track 4: Renewable Energy and Clean Technology

  • Solar and Wind Energy Systems
  • Energy Storage Solutions
  • Smart Grid Technologies
  • Bioenergy and Biofuels
  • Hydrogen Technology
  • Energy Management Systems

Track 5: Green Infrastructure and Sustainable Development

  • Sustainable Building Design
  • Green Construction Materials
  • Urban Planning and Development
  • Water Resource Management
  • Waste Management and Recycling
  • Circular Economy

Track 6: Healthcare Technology and Biomedical Engineering

  • Digital Health and Telemedicine
  • Medical Imaging and Diagnostics
  • Biomedical Signal Processing
  • Health Informatics
  • Wearable Health Devices
  • AI in Healthcare

Track 7: Climate-Responsive Innovation

  • Climate Change Mitigation Technologies
  • Environmental Monitoring Systems
  • Disaster Management and Early Warning
  • Sustainable Agriculture Technology
  • Biodiversity Conservation
  • Ocean and Marine Technology

Paper Submission Guidelines

  • Authors are invited to submit original, unpublished research papers
  • Papers must be written in English
  • All papers must follow the Springer manuscript preparation guidelines
  • Use standard single column conference templates
  • Only PDF files will be accepted for the review process
  • Short papers: 6-8 pages
  • Long papers: 10-12 pages
  • Maximum similarity allowed: 15% (excluding bibliography)
  • Maximum similarity from single source: 3%

Important Note

  • All papers will go through a rigorous double-blind reviewing process
  • At least one author of an accepted paper must register at the conference
  • All papers accepted and presented at the conference will be submitted for publication
  • Simultaneous submission to multiple conferences is not allowed

About Conference

The aim of ETMIS 2026 is to provide a premier interdisciplinary platform for researchers, academicians, industry professionals, and policy-makers to present and discuss the most recent innovations, trends, and practical challenges encountered in the fields of emerging technologies and their role in fostering sustainable development.
